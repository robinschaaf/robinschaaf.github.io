About Me

I thrive on helping people - and in my line of work this ends up being the end user whom I advocate for. I tend to view solutions that are more of an "And" than an "Or", and approach problems holistically with a primary goal of understanding reasoning of the end user (and/or business reason, as applicable).

But that is about as far as my brand of optimism goes - I'm very analytical (almost to a fault), and - perhaps out of sheer stubborness - don't give up on solving problems once determined to figure them out.

Perhaps my best asset is in growth-based mindset. I love learning new things all the time - improving the way I interact with others and learning about behavior, emotions and motivations. I have mostly worked in only two organizations throughout my thirteen year career, I've never stayed on the same job for more than a couple of years, often drastically changing my focus to new technologies and/or new stakeholders. As a result of this I've gained a lot of varied experience - from very large production oracle environments, to smaller workflow internal applications. Throughout, my best work has remained on helping others with their work.

Things I love to learn about

Some subjects really fascinate me and ultimately, I'd love for my work to somehow impact:
  • Learning about what motivates people, how emotions work and influence behavior (and vice-versa)
  • Pretty much anything relating to cognitive science or neuroscience
  • Developmental disorders - especially Autism and ADHD - and the use of technology/games for developing affect and ease learning - so much that I wrote a dissertation about this subject
  • The technology in our daily lives - things like nutrition, fitness, health, learning, entertainment, etc.
